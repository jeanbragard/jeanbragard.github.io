Analysis of time series of physiological data

I am interested in applying the tools of nonlinear dynamics to the analysis of physiological data. The advent of new experimental techniques in the field of medicine (like fMRI, NMR, positron tomography, etc.) has lead to the production of large amount of data with very high quality. The new challenge is to extract the relevant informations from these collections of data in order to improve diagnostics and to assess medical treatments.

In the figure on the right, you see that the integrated ECG corresponds to the sum of the electrical activity coming from the different myocytes forming the atria and ventricles. From the point of view of signal analysis, the ECG gives us an incomplete information from the status of the heart. To infer the "real" state of the heart from the ECG is indeed a mathematically "ill-posed" problem and it requires great care. In order to get better information from the ECG, I use new algorithms that are beyond the linear tools (Fourier analysis and correlation functions), like Lyapunov spectrum, signal entropy and fractal dimension.
 

For this project I am collaborating with the research group of Dr. Blas Echebarria at UPC (Barcelona) and Dr. Jorge Elorza (UNAV).
